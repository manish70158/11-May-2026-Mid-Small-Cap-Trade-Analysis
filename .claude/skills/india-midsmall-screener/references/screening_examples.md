# Screening Examples

This document provides practical examples of how to apply the screening methodology.

## Example 1: Complete Screening Process

Let's walk through screening a hypothetical stock "GROWTH Ltd." from Nifty Midcap 100:

### Step 1: Collect Data
- **Symbol**: GROWTH.NS
- **Current Price**: ₹850
- **52-Week High/Low**: ₹1,100 / ₹650
- **Market Cap**: ₹12,000 Cr
- **Sector**: Technology

### Step 2: Valuation Filters

**P/E Ratio Analysis**:
- Stock P/E: 18.5
- Technology Sector Avg P/E: 25.0
- Discount: 26% below sector average ✓ Pass (>20% discount)

**P/B and ROE Analysis**:
- P/B Ratio: 2.8
- ROE: 22%
- Criteria: P/B < 3 AND ROE > 15% ✓ Pass

**PEG Ratio**:
- P/E: 18.5
- EPS Growth Rate: 28%
- PEG: 18.5 / 28 = 0.66 ✓ Pass (<1)

**Price Position**:
- Current Price: ₹850
- 52-Week High: ₹1,100
- Position: 77% of 52W high ✓ Pass (60-85% range)

**Result**: GROWTH Ltd. passes all valuation filters.

### Step 3: Growth Assessment

**Historical Growth**:
- Revenue Growth (YoY): 32% ✓ Pass (>20%)
- EPS Growth (YoY): 28% ✓ Pass (>25%)
- Consistent over 2 years: Yes ✓

**Momentum**:
- Recent trend: Uptrend after consolidation ✓
- Volume on up days: Increasing ✓
- RSI: 52 ✓ Pass (40-60 range)

**Business Quality**:
- Operating Margin trend: 18% → 20% → 22% (improving) ✓
- Order Book: Strong visibility for next 2 quarters ✓
- Market position: Gaining share in cloud services ✓

**Sector Tailwinds**:
- Technology sector: Strong demand for digital transformation ✓
- Government initiatives: Digital India, PLI scheme support ✓

**Result**: Strong growth potential.

### Step 4: Risk Assessment

**Financial Health**:
- Debt-to-Equity: 0.3 ✓ Pass (<1)
- Interest Coverage: 8.5x ✓ Pass (>3x)
- Current Ratio: 1.8 ✓ Pass (>1.2)

**Liquidity**:
- Avg Daily Volume: 850K shares ✓ Pass (>500K for midcap)
- Market Cap: ₹12,000 Cr ✓ Pass (>₹5,000 Cr)

**Volatility**:
- Beta: 1.2 ✓ Pass (0.8-1.5)
- Max Drawdown (1Y): -25% ✓ Pass (<40%)

**Governance**:
- Promoter Holding: 58% (stable) ✓
- No corporate governance issues ✓

**Result**: Acceptable risk profile.

### Step 5: Composite Scoring

**Valuation Score (30 points)**:
- P/E discount: 8/10 (26% discount)
- P/B + ROE: 9/10 (excellent combination)
- PEG: 8/10 (0.66, strong)
- **Subtotal**: 25/30

**Growth Score (40 points)**:
- Historical growth: 14/15 (strong, consistent)
- Momentum: 9/10 (good technical setup)
- Business quality: 14/15 (improving margins, strong position)
- **Subtotal**: 37/40

**Risk Score (30 points)**:
- Financial health: 10/10 (excellent)
- Liquidity: 9/10 (good volume)
- Volatility: 9/10 (manageable)
- **Subtotal**: 28/30

**Total Score**: 90/100 → Strong candidate

### Step 6: Investment Thesis

**Why It Could Double in 6-12 Months**:
1. Currently trading at 26% discount to sector despite superior growth
2. Multiple expansion potential as market recognizes growth
3. Strong sector tailwinds with government support
4. Improving margins indicating operational leverage

**Target Price Calculation**:
- Fair P/E (sector average): 25
- Expected EPS (12M forward): ₹50
- Target Price: 25 × ₹50 = ₹1,250
- Current Price: ₹850
- Upside: 47% (conservative scenario)

- Optimistic P/E (premium for growth): 30
- Target: 30 × ₹50 = ₹1,500
- Upside: 76% (aggressive scenario)

**Key Catalysts**:
- Q3 earnings showing margin expansion
- Major client wins announcements
- Sector re-rating in tech stocks

## Example 2: Stock That Fails Screening

**DEBT Corp.** (Nifty Smallcap 100):

### Valuation Filters
- P/E: 12 vs Sector: 18 ✓ (33% discount)
- P/B: 0.8, ROE: 8% ✗ (ROE < 15%)
- PEG: 2.5 ✗ (>1)

**Result**: Fails valuation stage. Low P/E might be a value trap due to poor profitability.

**Why This Stock Fails**:
- Low ROE indicates capital inefficiency
- High PEG suggests expensive relative to growth
- Likely a value trap, not a growth opportunity

**VOLATILE Inc.** (Nifty Midcap 100):

Passes valuation and growth filters, but fails risk assessment:

**Risk Issues**:

## Red Flags to Watch For

Even if a stock passes filters, watch for these warning signs:

1. **One-time events inflating growth**: Check if revenue/EPS growth is sustainable or from asset sales
2. **Deteriorating margins**: Even with revenue growth, declining margins indicate competitive pressure
3. **Frequent management changes**: Instability in leadership
4. **Pending litigation**: Major legal issues can derail growth
5. **Promoter pledge**: High pledging of shares indicates financial stress
6. **Related party transactions**: Excessive RPTs can indicate governance issues
7. **Audit qualifications**: Red flag in financial statements

## Practical Tips

1. **Batch Processing**: Screen 20-30 stocks at once, then deep-dive into top 10
2. **Cross-verify Data**: Yahoo Finance data can have errors; cross-check key metrics
3. **Sector Rotation**: Consider current market phase - which sectors are in favor?
4. **News Check**: Before finalizing, check recent news for any red flags
5. **Peer Comparison**: Always compare to 2-3 direct competitors
6. **Management Commentary**: Read recent earnings call transcripts for forward guidance
7. **Institutional Activity**: Check if FIIs/DIIs are accumulating or distributing

---

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Data Lag**: Financial data can be 3-6 months old; factor in recent developments
2. **Seasonality**: Some businesses have seasonal patterns; check multiple quarters
3. **Circular References**: If using TTM (Trailing Twelve Months) data, ensure it's recent
4. **Index Effect**: Stocks recently added to indices may have artificial premium
5. **Small Sample**: One quarter of good results doesn't make a trend
6. **Confirmation Bias**: Don't force fit stocks into criteria; be objective
7. **Ignoring Macros**: Even great stocks underperform in bearish markets
